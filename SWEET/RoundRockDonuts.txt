Round Rock Donuts
(Modified from: https://basicswithbabish.co/basicsepisodes/donuts)


Ingredients

1 cup whole milk
1 tsp granulated sugar (for the yeast)
1 packet active dry yeast
350g all purpose flour
75g white sugar
1 tsp table salt
3 large egg yolks
Additional flour for work surface
Vegetable oil

+ For the chocolate glaze:
4 ounces chopped chocolate
1/4 cup whole milk
Dash of instant espresso powder
2 Tbsp corn syrup



+ For classic donut glaze:
400g powdered sugar
2 tsp light corn syup
1/4 cup boiling water



BLOOM YEAST:: Start by heating some milk in a bowl to 110°F and then add 1 tsp of sugar along with 1 packet of active dry yeast. Stir to combine and set aside for 10 minutes.


COMBINE DRY:: In a large bowl combine 350 grams of all purpose flour with 75 grams of white sugar, and 1 tsp of table salt. Whisk to combine.


ADD WET:: In the same bowl add 3 large egg yolks and the milk and yeast mixture. 


MIX:: Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix dough together until it forms a large shaggy mass.


KNEAD:: Turn out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8 minutes. Once dough is smooth, add to an oiled bowl, cover,


PROOF 1:: let sit for about an hour or until it’s doubled in size.


ROLL/CUT: Turn dough out onto a floured work surface, pat with a little bit of flour, and then roll out to about ½ inch thickness. Then use a biscuit cutter dusted with flour to cut the dough into donut rounds and place them on a rimmed floured baking sheet.


PROOF 2:: Cover and let rise for 45 minutes.


FRY:: Heat up vegetable oil in a cast iron skillet to 350°F. Add donuts to the oil and cook for about 45 seconds per side.


COOL:: Remove from oil and place on a wire rack to let cool.



----

For Classic Donut Glaze

In a bowl sift 400 grams of powdered sugar along with 2 tsp of corn syrup and ¼ cup of boiling water. Whisk until smooth.





For Chocolate Glaze

Combine 4 ounces of chopped chocolate with ¼ cup of steaming whole milk, a little instant espresso powder, and a couple tablespoons of corn syrup. Whisk to combine.
